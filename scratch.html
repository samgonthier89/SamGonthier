A Basic Introduction to Elves in Middle-Earth & their Contributions


For those unacquainted, you may find the elves to be snobbish, prudish, and disconnected with the world. However, I contend that they are noble and legendary!

As per the video there are three main kinds of elves in Middle Earth

  1. Vanyar:
      These are the elves most dedicated to the valar, and were the mightiest of them all. Upon arriving in Valinor (the homeland of the valar) they never left. They only returned to Beleriand and Arda (the Earth or Middle-Earth as we know it) during the war of wrath. They lived in the light. Their king was Ingwe who lives to this day.
  2. Vanyar:
      These are the deep elves. These elves made it to Valinor, but some of them rebelled and returned to Aman seeking after the silmarils (jewels of incredible value which held the light of the two tress of Valinor which had been destroyed by Morgoth and Ungoliant). These Noldor fell from grace and only were allowed to return after much sorrow. Their stories are among the most tragic of Tolkien's. Notable members of this group are people such as Elrond, Galadriel, Gil-Galad, Feanor and many more.
  3. Teleri:
      The Teleri are any of those who embarked on the journey to Valinor but never made it to the summits of Valinor like the others. This group is the most diverse as there are the Nandor, the falathrim, the elves of sirion and many more. (those who went back and gave up).

This information merely provides the most basic introduction to the elves. They have been prolific throughout the history of Arda and have played a role in every single period of history. Below you will find a visualization that shows the contribution of each member of the fellowship of the ring during the Lord of the Rings film. Pay attention to Legolas. Though he does not speak much, he left a mark on the world and was a major contributor to the salvation of the free-peoples of Middle-Earth.
